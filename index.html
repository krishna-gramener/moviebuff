Content Guidelines

Country-specific content censoring and rating guidelines for video content review.

India - CBFC Guidelines

Rating Categories

U (Universal) - Suitable for all ages
U/A 7+ - Parental guidance for children under 7
U/A 13+ - Parental guidance for children under 13
U/A 16+ - Parental guidance for children under 16
A (Adults Only) - Restricted to adults (18+)

Content Restrictions

  • Graphic violence and animal cruelty require blurring or pixelation
  • Strong profanity must be beeped or muted for television broadcast
  • Sexual content and nudity are heavily restricted
  • Religious and cultural sensitivities must be respected
  • Content warnings required for graphic scenes
  • Watershed hours (post-11 PM) for adult content on TV

Key Considerations

  • Family viewing is prioritized in Indian culture
  • Conservative broadcast standards apply
  • Digital platforms have more flexibility with age verification
  • Regional language content may have additional considerations

United States - TV Parental Guidelines

Rating Categories

TV-Y - All children
TV-Y7 - Directed to older children (7+)
TV-G - General audience
TV-PG - Parental guidance suggested
TV-14 - Parents strongly cautioned (14+)
TV-MA - Mature audiences only (17+)

Content Descriptors

  • D - Suggestive dialogue
  • L - Coarse or crude language
  • S - Sexual situations
  • V - Violence
  • FV - Fantasy violence (for children's programming)

Broadcast Requirements

  • FCC safe-harbor hours (10 PM - 6 AM) for explicit content
  • Rating bug must be displayed for first 15 seconds of each program
  • Content advisories for graphic material
  • Premium cable and streaming have more flexibility

France - CSA Guidelines

Rating Categories

Tous publics - All audiences
-10 - Not recommended for children under 10
-12 - Not recommended for children under 12
-16 - Not recommended for children under 16
-18 - Prohibited for minors

Content Philosophy

  • Emphasis on artistic and educational merit
  • More permissive toward nudity in artistic contexts
  • Authentic nature content valued over excessive editing
  • Documentary standards prioritize unfiltered reality
  • Cultural and intellectual content given consideration

Broadcast Standards

  • Age-rating logos must be visible throughout broadcast
  • Time-slot restrictions for youth protection
  • Warnings for potentially disturbing content
  • Respect for French language and cultural values

Japan - Eirin & Broadcasting Standards

Rating Categories

G - General audiences
PG12 - Parental guidance for under 12
R15+ - Restricted to 15 and above
R18+ - Restricted to adults only

Content Restrictions

  • Mosaic blur required for graphic blood and violence
  • Animal-related content requires cultural sensitivity
  • Strong language must be moderated or warned
  • Explicit content heavily regulated
  • Preference for edited over raw content
  • Late-night time slots for mature content

Cultural Considerations

  • High viewer sensitivity to graphic imagery
  • Respect for traditional values and aesthetics
  • Clear warnings and context required
  • Age-gate verification for online distribution
  • Post-midnight broadcast for extreme content

Stage-by-Stage Architecture + Technologies

2.1 Content Ingestion Layer

What Happens: Frontend sends request, Video uploaded to storage (entry point)

Technologies: Amazon S3, API Gateway, AWS Lambda

2.2 Event Trigger Layer

What Happens: Upload triggers pipeline automatically

Technologies: S3 Event Notifications, (Optional) SQS, EventBridge

2.3 AI Processing Pipeline

What Happens: Metadata extraction, Audio transcription, AI analysis, Rating generation

Technologies:
• Compute: Lambda / ECS / EKS
• AI Services: Amazon Transcribe, Amazon Rekognition, Amazon Comprehend

2.4 Intermediate Data Storage

What Happens: Stores transcripts, labels, analysis outputs

Technologies: Amazon S3

2.5 Aggregation Layer

What Happens: Combines intermediate outputs into final result

Technologies: AWS Lambda / ECS

2.6 Final Data Storage

What Happens: Stores final structured AI results

Technologies: Amazon DynamoDB

2.7 Backend API Layer

What Happens: Fetches results for frontend

Technologies: API Gateway, AWS Lambda

2.8 Frontend Layer

What Happens: Displays results and video

Technologies: React (or similar), HLS Streaming

2.9 Video Streaming Layer

What Happens: Converts video to streaming format

Technologies: HLS, Amazon S3, (Optional) CloudFront
